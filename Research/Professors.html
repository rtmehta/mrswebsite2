Featured Professor: Suneel Kodambaka

Professor Kodambaka and his lab currently have two main projects: the synthesis of thin film materials and mechanical behavior analysis of high-temperature ceramics.

Synthesis of thin film materials is primarily accomplished by way of chemical vapor deposition (CVD) and hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) is the material most commonly produced through CVD in the lab. Professor Kodambakaâ€™s lab is interested in h-BN as it offers a variety of potential uses, whether that be in computer chips or as a lubricant. h-Bn and the other thin films produced in the Kodambaka lab are analyzed by various tools and methods, including scanning tunneling microscopy.

Mechanical behavior analysis of high-temperature ceramics is when ceramic crystals are grown at high temperature and subsequently tested after cooling back to room temperature. This process alters the characteristics of the ceramic crystals allowing them to exhibit properties of other materials, most notably metals, while maintaining the qualities of a ceramic material.
